The Last Dragon's Flight

A tale of bone, wind, and ancient skies

In ages long forgotten by mortal tongues, when the sky was a darker canvas and stars burned brighter than the sharpest blade, there existed creatures of such magnificent terror that entire kingdoms were shaped around their migrations. The dragons were not merely beasts of fire and scale — they were living storms, sentient hurricanes wearing the armour of mountains.

Aerindal, the last of the Cinderwing line, remembered the old paths. She traced them not by map nor by compass, but by the pull of magnetic rivers buried deep within the world's bones, currents invisible to every creature save her kind. Above the Serenthal Peaks she turned west, banking on thermals that rose like invisible pillars from the sun-scorched stone below, her vast wings making no sound but the deep resonant hum that villagers three valleys over would later describe as distant thunder.

Her scales had long since shifted from the ember-orange of her youth into a deep wine-red, threaded with veins of darkened copper where the centuries had left their mark. Each scar told a story — a crossbow bolt from the Siege of Valdenmoor, a sword slash earned from the great duel above the Drowned Sea. To look upon Aerindal in flight was to read the history of a world, written in broken scales and star-lit breath.

Now, as the sun fell behind the Teeth of Lorn, Aerindal smelled the iron scent of forgotten magic rising from the rift below. The old bones of the earth whispered, and she answered with a cry that shattered the evening clouds.
